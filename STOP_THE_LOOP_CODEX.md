## Part I — Mechanism Foundations

### 1) Predictive Nervous System (PNS)

**Definition**  
The brain’s default operating system that continuously generates predictions about sensory input, emotional outcomes, and future events to reduce uncertainty and conserve energy.

**Core Concepts**
- Prediction = expectation formed before outcome
- Prediction error = mismatch between expectation and reality
- Prediction lock = persistent prediction that does not resolve
- Activation = physiological mobilization in response to prediction error

**Metaphor**  
**Predictive Radar** — The mind locks onto a future target and keeps scanning until it detects confirmation.

**Content Prompts**
- Explain how prediction evolved to reduce metabolic cost
- Describe how prediction manifests in modern stress
- Provide example: anticipating a meeting outcome
- Provide example: replaying a conversation

### 2) Loop Formation

**Definition**  
A feedback cycle formed when a prediction fails to resolve, causing repetitive internal simulation (rumination, worry, mental replay).

**Components**
- Unfinished stimulus
- Emotional activation
- Narrative recruitment
- Repetition

**Metaphor**  
**Irradiated Lens** — A tint placed over perception that alters interpretation without the wearer noticing.

**Content Prompts**
- Differentiate loop vs memory
- Identify common triggers (uncertainty, rejection, performance evaluation, loss of control)
- Diagram the loop cycle
- Provide night rumination example

### 3) Irradiated Lens

**Definition**  
A perceptual distortion filter created by conditioning, unresolved prediction loops, and survival bias.

**Functional Explanation**  
The brain does not record reality objectively. It interprets incoming data through prior predictive models.

**Metaphor**  
**Tinted Glass** — Wearing sunglasses indoors changes the color of everything, even if forgotten.

**Content Prompts**
- Explain how early conditioning forms lens patterns
- List common lenses: approval, threat, abandonment, control
- Differentiate lens vs belief vs personality

## Part II — Loop Detection and Mapping

### 4) Loop Identification Protocol

**Definition**  
A repeatable method for recognizing when a person is inside a predictive rumination loop.

**Steps**
1. Notice physiological activation
2. Identify repeating thought pattern
3. Track duration
4. Observe emotional charge
5. Confirm recurrence

**Common Signals**
- Tight chest
- Racing thoughts
- Restless energy
- Mental rehearsal

**Metaphor**  
**Red Alert System** — The body alarms before the mind explains.

**Content Prompts**
- Teach body-first awareness
- Explain neutral observation
- Provide real-time detection example

### 5) Causal Inquiry (Why-as-Cause)

**Definition**  
Structured questioning used to identify the cause of activation rather than assign blame.

**Rules**
- Why = What caused this activation?
- No moral language
- Stop at physiological recognition
- Avoid narrative expansion

**Descent Model**  
Trigger → Reaction → Meaning → Fear → Original Conditioning Pattern

**Metaphor**  
**Onion Peeling** — Descending layer by layer until core sensation emerges.

**Content Prompts**
- Provide scripted example dialogue
- Differentiate why-as-blame vs why-as-cause
- Define stop condition

## Part III — Regulation and Dissipation

### 6) Regulation Principles

**Definition**  
Methods used to reduce physiological activation so narrative recruitment loses energy.

**Principles**
- Slow breath regulates vagal tone
- Body anchoring interrupts simulation
- Dissipation ≠ suppression

**Breath Protocol Example**
- 4-second inhale
- 4-second hold
- 6-second exhale
- Repeat 5 cycles

**Metaphor**  
**Volume Knob** — Lowering amplitude without turning off the system.

**Content Prompts**
- Explain sympathetic vs parasympathetic states
- Contrast suppression vs dissipation
- Provide step-by-step calming sequence

### 7) Loop Dissipation Protocol

**Definition**  
Structured interruption process to dissolve predictive rumination.

**Steps**
1. Notice activation
2. Regulate breath
3. Anchor attention to sensation
4. Identify defended prediction
5. Release expectation
6. Return to baseline

**Metaphor**  
**River Bank Model** — Energy flows when not resisted; eddies form when trapped.

**Content Prompts**
- Walkthrough scenario: argument replay
- Walkthrough scenario: night rumination
- Define baseline state
